🚀 First 30 Days Fix Plan

Rapid stabilization to fix immediate structural issues before the full roadmap begins.

Week 1 — Emergency Audit
Urgent
  • Hold an All-Executives Emergency Meeting (in-person or online)
  • Distribute Role Clarity Worksheet — each exec writes their job
  • Identify top 3 pain points by vote
  • Assign a temporary 'Point Person' for each pain point
  • Audit current membership list: active vs. inactive
  • Review existing social media engagement rates & brand consistency
Week 2 — Role Restructuring
Structure
  • Present official Role Descriptions (Part 2) to every executive
  • Have each executive sign a Responsibility Agreement
  • Create WhatsApp/Telegram Executives Group with clear rules
  • Set up shared Google Drive: Events / Academic / Finances / Media
  • Set up Weekly Check-In: every Sunday, each exec submits 3-line update
  • Create Club Dashboard (Google Sheet) for tracking
Week 3 — Quick Wins
Visibility
  • Post the first 5 math problems on Facebook immediately
  • Run a free 'Math Challenge of the Week' open to all school members
  • Announce the club via school notice board and social media
  • Set date for first proper club session
  • Begin contacting at least 2 potential sponsors or partner schools
Week 4 — Systems Launch
Culture
  • Hold the first official Weekly Club Session
  • Formally introduce the Reward System
  • Publish the Monthly Newsletter on Facebook
  • Review Weeks 1–3 with executives: what worked, what didn't
  • Set KPI targets for Month 2
  • Draft and share 3-month mini-roadmap with members
🎯 30-Day Goal: By day 30, every executive should know exactly what they do, the club should have published content online, one club session should have happened, and a tracking system should be running.

Manage club calendar. Coordinate with school timetable. Book venues and arrange permissions. Maintain budget tracking sheet. Handle procurement of materials.

Photograph and film all club sessions and events. Edit and deliver photos within 48 hours. Create short video reels for Facebook. Maintain photo/video archive in Google Drive.

🎨 Creativity & Innovation

Going beyond equations — connecting math to stories, art, real-world problems, and technology.

01
Math Storytelling Project
Students write short stories (300–500 words) where the plot hinges on a mathematical concept. Best stories published in the magazine and on social media. Example: a mystery solved through modular arithmetic.
02
Visual Math Gallery
Students create visual art inspired by math: fractals, geometric patterns, tessellations. Gallery displayed at the Math Fair. Best works featured on social media.
03
Real-Life Math Projects
Groups of 3–4 students tackle real-world problems using math. Examples: optimizing school bus routes, analyzing canteen queue patterns, studying population growth. Final presentations at the Math Fair.
04
Math + Technology
Students create simple math games or visualizations using free tools (GeoGebra, Desmos, Scratch). Best projects showcased at the Annual Gala.
05
Student-Led Initiative Program
Any member can propose a creative math initiative. Submit a 1-page proposal to the VP. If approved, the student leads the initiative with exec support — building leadership among non-exec members.

⚠️ Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

10 mistakes that kill clubs — and exactly how BAFSK Math Club avoids every one of them.

#1
No Clear Role Assignment
❌ Why it kills: Executives don't know what to do — everything falls on 1–2 people and they burn out.
✅ Fix: Use the Role Cards in Part 2. Sign a responsibility agreement on day 1.
#2
Planning Without Executing
❌ Why it kills: Beautiful plans exist in documents no one looks at.
✅ Fix: Every plan must have a named owner, deadline, and weekly check-in.
#3
Inconsistent Social Media
❌ Why it kills: Page goes dead for 2 weeks — followers forget the club exists.
✅ Fix: Use the weekly content calendar. Pre-schedule posts using Buffer or Meta Business Suite.
#4
Only Targeting 'Smart' Students
❌ Why it kills: Club becomes elitist. Most students feel excluded. Membership drops.
✅ Fix: Run Track 1 sessions always. Make fun, accessible events monthly.
#5
No Member Feedback System
❌ Why it kills: Problems fester. Members leave quietly. Exec doesn't know why.
✅ Fix: Send a 5-min monthly survey. Act on the results publicly.
#6
Ignoring Non-Olympiad Members
❌ Why it kills: 90% of members aren't in the Olympiad squad and feel irrelevant.
✅ Fix: Run creative events, fun competitions, storytelling — not just hardcore math.
#7
Poor Leadership Handover
❌ Why it kills: When senior execs graduate, institutional knowledge disappears.
✅ Fix: Document everything. Identify successors by Month 10. Shadow program in Months 11–12.
#8
Events With No Promotion
❌ Why it kills: Low attendance despite hard work. Demoralizes organizers.
✅ Fix: Start marketing any event 3 weeks before. Use all channels: Facebook, school board, word-of-mouth.
#9
No Tracking of Results
❌ Why it kills: Club can't see if it's growing or shrinking.
✅ Fix: Use the KPI dashboard weekly. Monthly review is non-negotiable.
#10
Executive Burnout
❌ Why it kills: Trying to do too much with no rest. Quality drops, resentment builds.
✅ Fix: Distribute work genuinely. Say no to events that overload the team. Quality over quantity.

03
The Open Session Model
Once per month, run a completely FREE open-to-all session with no membership required. Format: 'Math Kahoot', 'Fastest Solver Wins'. This is your main recruitment funnel.
04
Inter-School Network
After Month 6, reach out to 3–5 other schools to form a Bangladesh Schools Math Network. Share problems and host mini-competitions. Host the Inter-School Invitational in Month 11.
05
Online Community
Create a Facebook Group for members — active discussion and peer help. Pin a 'Problem of the Day' daily. Recognize top contributors monthly with public appreciation.
06
Alumni Network
Keep in touch with graduates — they become mentors, guest speakers, and sponsors. Create an Alumni WhatsApp group by Month 8. Invite alumni to events and the magazine.
